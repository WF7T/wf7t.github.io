November is busy contest time! CW SS come and gone at Kirk K4RO's. Fun operation, netting 808 Qs between us under the call K4TCG. I operated at Kirk's on Saturday for the start, then on Sunday I operated under my own call/station for another 375Qs as "fresh meat". I did a casual effort in the SSB SS. I am not practiced at SSB contesting, and I tip my hat to all of the fantastic SSB contest operators I heard/worked. I also did a single-band effort in CQWW CW, having fun expecially with propogation to JA much improved. I was hoping to be able to work/confirm zone 37 to complete WAZ for 15, but the pile-up too deep and me too weak. Lastly, I got to play a little bit in the ARRL 160 contest. I was not able to put BIC (butt-in-chair) for the entire contest, so I had to settle with two two-hour sessions on Saturday evening.

Next contests I am looking forward to are the ARRL 10M and Stew Perry Top-band Distance Challenge. 

Had some nice confirmations to my DXCC, WAZ, and WPX totals as a result of this fall's DXpeditions to the African continent. I am inching my way toward WAZ award of excellence, with only 8 additional African prefixes needed. With my recent award of WAZ, I am now eligable for the 5BWAZ since I have 167 of 200 confirmed. As always, I appreciate the intrepid DXers who travel to far-away (and sometimes dangerous) lands to give DX nerds like me a chance to contact them.

I am delayed in doing any additional antenna work. I started a new job so I have been focused upon that priority instead of radio. I did manage to cobble together a feedbox for my full-time Inverted L but I am not happy with it. I am currently in my 160M winter configuration: My rebuilt G5RV voltage-fed as a Marconi over 1500 feet total on-ground radials, using an autotuner. I should have one of the two 40M dipoles up on the trees in the next week or so.
